ABOUT US

Established in 2001, the Hussaini Youth Federation in Ladakh and Srinagar has emerged as a steadfast community for Ladakhi students navigating their educational journey in Srinagar. Rooted in the Shia Islamic tradition, the federation holds a primary focus on organizing religious events, particularly Majalis Aza and Eid Majalis, providing a spiritual anchor for the students away from their native land. As a nucleus of Shia Islamic heritage, the federation plays a pivotal role in preserving and promoting the cultural identity of Ladakhi students. The significance of its establishment in 2001 lies in the subsequent years of commitment to fostering a sense of unity and shared faith among the students. These religious events serve not only as occasions for celebration but also as moments of connection and reflection, allowing the students to maintain a strong bond with their religious roots. In the tapestry of Ladakhi student life in Srinagar, the Hussaini Youth Federation acts as a vital thread, weaving together the diverse cultural fabric of Ladakh and Srinagar. The federation's existence is a testament to the resilience and determination of the students to stay connected with their religious traditions, even while pursuing education away from home. Beyond the realm of religious events, the federation has become a support system for Ladakhi students. It serves as a bridge between the two distinct cultural landscapes, fostering understanding and solidarity among the youth. The year 2001 marks the beginning of a journey where the federation has played a crucial role in supporting Ladakhi students, offering a sense of belonging, and contributing positively to the broader community.
